Rules and
Regulations

Essay writing entails much more than an academic endeavour. It is a thought provoking-venture that engages both the reader and the writer. YESM provides a diverse platform that encourages these promising writers from all fields of study to showcase their literary prowess.

Topics:

  1. Is India on a sustainable path to societal and economic progress?
  2. Increase in female labour force participation post-liberalisation: A baby step or a giant stride in women‚Äôs empowerment.
  3. Cyber-feminism in India: Role of social media movements in women‚Äôs empowerment and gender equality.

Rules and Format:

  1. Participants must be enrolled undergraduate students of any department.
  2. The essay must be an individual work of the author. No collaborations are allowed, and one participant can submit only one essay.
  3. Participants must write an essay on any one of the given topics.
  4. The essay must not exceed a word count of 1600 words (including in-text citations but excluding cover page, footnotes, diagram captions, tables (if any), and end-text citations).
  5. The first page of the essay page must mention the title of the essay and the participant number.
  6. Participants should use the APA referencing style to cite other papers and articles.
  7. Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.

Not abiding by the aforementioned rules or format may lead to disqualification.
